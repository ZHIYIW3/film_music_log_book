Making Music for Film and Animation 1 - Week 2 Log Book

Lecture Content and Reflection

Assignment 1 Introduction

  • How many minutes is the music used in the film? Percentage? Was there some silence?
  • What are the different approaches used in the film?
  • Need to discuss both films and do some comparisons.
  • What is the inspiration for music (aesthetic, arrangement, creative choices)?
  • Discuss the positive and negative in both of the films.

Elements of music

  • Melody: ways of creating a theme, any collection of notes, something you can harm
    • Melody can be consonant (notes in the harmony) or dissonant (notes sound weird)
    • Some composers like to have their own theme and their own sound. Nowadays, most composers all sound the same → commercial sound.
  • Rhythm: sense of propulsion and syncopation (off-beat), give the pace of music and film
  • Harmony: chords support the melody → chord progression
  • Tempo: the speed of music, would be different according to what happens in the scene
  • Form: Different sections of the music, not some key things need to focus on film music
  • Dynamics: how loud/quiet the music, really important for the film music (the best way is to do some volume automation on the track while watching the film)
  • Timbre: instrumentation was used for the arrangement of the music
  • Texture: how many layers you have in the music
  • The most important thing for film music is what does the right thing for the scene instead of focusing on the rule

Interstellar

  • The dynamics and texture change over the storyline. As the story develops, the music is gradually getting louder, more instruments and layers are added in.
  • A theme can be heard from the cut scene. There is a basic pattern but lots of variation happens according to what’s on the screen.
  • Most of the notes used are within the harmony. However, some of the dissonance was used to make the audience feel nervous.
  • A Long note was used for the ending of a section of the music.
  • The use of silence followed by a loud noise makes a powerful contrast, force the audience to focus on what happened on the screen.
