Making Music for Film and Animation 1 - Week 1 Log Book

Lecture Content and Reflection

What is film music?

  • Music can change the perception of what happening on the screen.
  • Music can help enhance themes, characters, emotions, and everything that happens in the film.
  • Help reinforce the emotional impact of the scene.
  • Sometimes, silence can say a lot more than music.
  • Help viewers understand the stories and characters.

1896-1926 (silent year of film)

  • Do not have the technology to make music layers on software and be added to film
  • The beginning of cinema - ‘Arrival of a Train at La Ciotat” (1896)
    • Only environment sound - can hear the sound of trains and people
    • First publicly exhibited motion picture
  • “The Story of the Kelly Gain” (1906)
    • First silent feature-length film in the world
    • Has a critical and commercial success
    • Dramatic breast music would work
  • Do not be restricted to one of the options when doing the music.
  • The reason that film music started is to distract the audience from the sound of projectors.
  • “The Cure” (1917) - Charlie Chaplin
    • What is perceived to be the soundtrack of every black-and-white movie
    • A solo piano playing along the movie
    • A live-action/improvising for the movie - play the popular tune of the days
    • Reusing the same theme for similar scenes or characters
    • Ensemble sizes depended on the economics of the picture theatres
    • Some piano and flute can be heard in the film
  • Intellectual Reference - linking pre-existing musical or pop culture knowledge from the ‘real world’ inside of a film’s pretend world
  • Listen to the music in the style that we know, makes us more involved in the show
