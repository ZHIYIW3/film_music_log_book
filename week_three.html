Making Music for Film and Animation 1 - Week 3 Log Book

Lecture Content and Reflection

Film and Animation Production Cycle

  • Pre-production: director doing script, treatment
  • Production: shot, storyboard
  • Post-production: editing, music
  • Back in before, the composer usually joined in the post-production process. Most of the thing has been down, they need to add music for the film.
  • Nowadays, the composer usually going to be involved in a much earlier stage. Sometimes, the director even asks the composer to write something based on the script.
  • The director and editor would use temp music to get the feeling of cutting scenes.
  • The earlier composers came in, they would get more of their own sound. Editors and directors do not need to use temp music any more.
  • ADR - Re-record of dialogue.

Diegetic and non-diegetic music

  • Diegetic music - Music can be heard in the film, what actually happened in the film, what the character can hear
  • Non-diegetic music - film music the character cannot hear, scored music

Film Music History - 1927 to 1945

  • Between 1930-1933 the majority of mainstream Hollywood films adhered to strict rules of diegetic music only accompanying film.
  • The Blue Angel 1930:
    • Heavy music was used in the opening but immediately stopped when the film started. All the scene later on is diegetic sound only.
    • The whistle sound from the character and the ringing sound from the bell increase the entertainment of this no-score music film.
  • King kong 1933
    • There is a page with ‘OVERTURE’ at the start of the film for quite a long time with the music, followed by some instructions, just like what happens to the opera or stage show. The overture gives audiences a basic idea of this film's overall mood and emotion.
    • The music gradually faded out when the actual film came in as well.
